What is Bennu?

101955 Bennu is a carbonaceous asteroid that measures a mean diameter of approximately 492 meters and travelling approximately 101,388.67 kilometers per hour through space.

Comprised of large amounts of carbon-based materials originating from dying stars such as red giants and supernovae, Bennu could contain a wide assortment of different rocks, minerals, precious metals, and potentially water.

Where is Bennu going?

At the time of its discovery on September 11, 1999, Bennu was about 2.2 million kilometers from the Earth. Bennu passes in close proximity to Earth approximately once every six years.

In 2010, dynamical study by Andrea Milani and collaborators predicted a series of eight potential Earth impacts by Bennu between 2169 and 2199. With a 1-in-2,700 chance of impacting Earth, Bennu is considered a potentially hazardous asteroid (PHA).
